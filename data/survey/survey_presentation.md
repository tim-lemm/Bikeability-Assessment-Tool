# Survey Presentation

---

This file is a presentation of the survey used for the study.

The survey component elicits users' perceptions of the bikeability of infrastructure segments and the relative importance
they attribute to specific characteristics. Because perceived safety and comfort are inherently subjective and context-dependent,
the survey is designed to be adaptable to different cities and inclusive of both cyclists and non-cyclists. The survey consist 
of the following components:


## General Questionnaire Structure

The survey is divided into **four main distinct parts**:
1. **Respondent Profile (Demographics & Habits):** Background information and current mobility patterns
2. **Photo-Based Evaluation (17 real-world scenarios):** Rating specific, concrete street setups in Lyon, France
3. **Feature Trade-offs (Best-Worst Scaling):** Prioritizing and ranking infrastructure criteria
4. **Open Comments:** Qualitative feedback regarding safety experiences and suggestions for improvement

---

## 1. Respondent Profile

### Demographics and Activity
To categorize the participants, several mandatory questions are asked:
* **Age:** Selection by brackets (Under 18, 18-24, 25-34, 35-49, 50-64, 65-69, 70-74, 75-85, Over 85 years old)
* **Gender:** Female, Male, Other, Prefer not to say
* **Location:** Residential zip code
* **Occupation:**
  * Artisans, Shopkeepers, Farmers
  * Business Owners / Executives
  * Managers, Higher Intellectual Professions
  * Intermediate Professions
  * Employees / Office Workers
  * Blue-collar Workers
  * Students
  * Retirees
  * Other

### Cycling Habits

This section evaluates the respondent's relationship with and reliance on bicycles:
* **Frequency of Use:** Every day or almost every day, one or several times a week/month, less than once a month, never
* **Purpose of Travel:** Commuting (Home to Work/School), Leisure, Other
* **Bicycle Ownership:** Owns a personal bicycle (Yes / Non)
* **Type of Bicycle (Propulsion & Service):** Personal mechanical bike, Personal e-bike, Bike-sharing mechanical, 
Bike-sharing electric, I do not ride a bicycle
* **Bicycle Category Used:** Road bike, Mountain bike (VTT), City bike (Dutch style), Folding bike, Cargo or Long-tail 
bike, Recumbent bike, Other

## 2. Visual Evaluation Methodology

* **Principle:** Respondents are asked to imagine themselves in the exact conditions shown in each photo, riding a 
bicycle in the direction of the camera view.
* **Rating Scale:** From 1 to 5 stars:
  * **5 stars:** *"I feel safe and comfortable riding a bike here"*
  * **1 star:** *"I would avoid riding a bike here"*
* **Diversity of Environments:** 17 photos showcasing actual infrastructures across the Lyon Metropolitan Area, varying 
in cycling track type, incline, vehicular speed limits, and surrounding greenery.

## 3. Feature Importance Analysis (Best-Worst Scaling)

In this section, the survey implements a discrete choice method known as **Best-Worst Scaling** to measure the relative 
weight of **5 key attributes** across 10 randomized triplet blocks. For each block, the user must select exactly one 
**most important** feature and one **least important** feature to make a route pleasant to cycle on:

1. **The slope of the road** (topography)
2. **The speed of cars** driving on the road (20, 30, or 50 km/h)
3. **The number of car lanes** (density of motorized traffic)
4. **The type of cycling track** (painted cycle lane, greenway, protected "Voie Lyonnaise" highway, etc.)
5. **The surrounding environment** (presence of greenery, trees, parks)

### Choice Blocks

Respondents systematically arbitrate between criteria via the following combinations:
* **Block 1:** Slope | Car speed | Number of car lanes
* **Block 2:** Slope | Car speed | Type of cycling track
* **Block 3:** Slope | Car speed | Surrounding environment (greenery)
* **Block 4:** Slope | Number of car lanes | Surrounding environment (greenery)
* **Block 5:** Slope | Type of cycling track | Surrounding environment (greenery)
* **Block 6:** Car speed | Number of car lanes | Type of cycling track
* **Block 7:** Car speed | Number of car lanes | Surrounding environment (greenery)
* **Block 8:** Car speed | Type of cycling track | Surrounding environment (greenery)
* **Block 9:** Number of car lanes | Type of cycling track | Surrounding environment (greenery)
* **Block 10:** Slope | Number of car lanes | Type of cycling track

## 4. Open-ended Feedback and Qualitative Insights

The survey concludes with an open qualitative window to capture real-world user experiences:
* **Urban Cycling Practice:** General thoughts regarding current cycling facilities and the overall experience of navigating a city by bike.
* **Desired Improvements:** *"What would you change and why?"*
* **Road Safety Context:** *"Have you ever been involved in dangerous situations?"*
